Integration¶

As the derivative of \(\sin x\) is \(\cos x\), and that of \(\cos x\) is \(-\sin x\), we have

\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned}\int_{a}^{b}\cos x dx = \sin b - \sin a\\\int_{a}^{b}\sin x dx = \cos a - \cos b\end{aligned}\end{align} \]

Isn’t that beautiful?
